Threat Analysis: "Anti-Noah's Ark" Coalition

This section details the adversary, a locally-based, ideologically motivated extremist group. Understanding their composition, capabilities, and probable courses of action is critical for effective counter-operations.

Adversary Profile

  • Identity: Loosely organized, socially isolated cells.
  • Composition: Small, decentralized, no formal command structure.
  • Ideology: Rejects community authority; seeks to "remove happiness" from perceived aggressors.
  • Limitations: Limited logistics and sustainment capacity; vulnerable to community tip-offs.

Adversary Capabilities

Probable Enemy Courses of Action (COA)

  1. Coordinated Multi-Point Arson: Simultaneous ignitions in WUI zones to overwhelm response and create chaos.
  2. Infrastructure Sabotage: Attacks on power, water, or comms nodes to hinder emergency response and sow panic.
  3. Disinformation Operations: Use of social media and word-of-mouth to spread false reports and incite public confusion.
  4. Concealment and Withdrawal: Use of terrain and local knowledge to evade capture post-attack.

Operational Plan: Phased Response & COAs

Phase 0: Prevention & Posture

  • Community engagement and suspicious activity reporting campaign.
  • Pre-stage resources in high-risk areas; update mutual aid agreements.
  • Conduct vulnerability assessments for critical infrastructure nodes.

Phase 1: Detection

  • Automate fire watch and critical node monitoring (drones, remote sensors).
  • Establish real-time incident reporting through unified comms.
  • Deploy intelligence teams for threat tracking.

Phase 2: Response

  • Dynamic asset deployment based on evolving threat picture.
  • Activate rumor control and public messaging team.
  • Coordinate law enforcement and fire/EMS for rapid containment and suspect apprehension.

Phase 3: Recovery

  • Begin damage assessment and critical infrastructure restoration.
  • Maintain public information flow and community reassurance.
  • Debrief, after-action reviews, and plan refinement.

Key Courses of Action (COAs)

  • Rapid multi-agency deployment to ignition points.
  • Use of aerial assets for fire mapping and suppression.
  • Mutual aid activation for surge response.
  • Harden key infrastructure with physical barriers and surveillance.
  • Deploy quick-repair teams for comms and power restoration.
  • Maintain backup communications and power for EOCs.
  • Activate social media monitoring and rumor control cell.
  • Push verified information through trusted channels only.
  • Counter disinformation with rapid, transparent updates.
